# Goats and Sheep (Small Ruminants)

## Role in Smallholder Systems
Small ruminants are an integral part of rural livelihoods and culture in Nigeria. The Red Sokoto breed is a dominant goat breed in many smallholder systems, kept mostly under semi-intensive management (partial confinement combined with grazing/browsing).

## Major Health Challenges
- **Helminthiasis (internal worms):** reported as the single most common disease issue affecting goats in smallholder studies — regular deworming is one of the highest-value health interventions available.
- **Peste des Petits Ruminants (PPR):** a highly contagious viral disease related to rinderpest, and likely the most important cause of early mortality, especially in goats. PPR can be effectively and economically controlled through vaccination (e.g. PPR vaccines such as those branded Ovivax PPR, registered for use in Nigeria).
- Limited access to veterinary services is a major constraint — many farmers rely on herbal/traditional treatment and only consult a vet as a last resort, which can delay effective treatment of serious disease.

## Feeding
- Common smallholder practice includes feeding kitchen waste and crop by-products such as cassava peels, which are cheap and locally available but nutritionally incomplete on their own.
- Cut-and-carry browse feeding (collecting tree/shrub leaves to bring to penned animals) and grazed fallow systems are both used in Nigerian small ruminant systems; alley farming (planting browse trees/shrubs in rows alongside crops) is one way to integrate feed production with cropping.
- Nutrition is, alongside disease, one of the two most important factors limiting small ruminant productivity — even where disease is controlled, undernourished animals will have poor growth and reproduction.

## Practical Priorities for a Smallholder
1. Deworm regularly (cost-effective, addresses the most common health complaint).
2. Vaccinate against PPR where the disease is present regionally.
3. Provide some form of housing/shelter, even basic, to reduce environmental stress and parasite exposure — many smallholder operations currently provide minimal shelter.
4. Supplement kitchen-waste/crop-residue feeding with better quality forage or browse where possible, rather than relying on residues alone.

*Sources: smallholder sheep and goat management studies in Anambra and Ogun States, Nigeria; FAO "Sheep and Goats in the Farming System" (ILCA Small Ruminant Programme); Nigeria Federal Ministry of Information livestock vaccine initiatives.*
