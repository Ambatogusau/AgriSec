# Cowpea Production (Nigeria)

## Why It Was Added
Cowpea appears repeatedly as the intercrop partner for maize, millet, sorghum, and groundnut across Nigerian smallholder systems, and is itself a major legume crop and protein source. Including it directly (rather than only as a footnote in other crops' documents) lets the assistant answer cowpea-specific questions.

## Importance
Cowpea is a key grain legume for food and income across the Nigerian savanna, valued both for its grain and for fixing nitrogen that benefits companion or rotation cereal crops.

## Pests
The most damaging pest is the **pod borer (Maruca vitrata)**, attacking flowers and pods, alongside aphids (the same Aphis craccivora that affects groundnut) and storage weevils (Callosobruchus maculatus) post-harvest.

## Practical Notes
- Intercropping cowpea with cereals (maize, millet, sorghum) is a long-standing practice that improves overall land-use efficiency and soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
- Post-harvest storage losses to bruchid weevils can be severe; hermetic storage (airtight bags/containers, e.g. PICS bags) is a widely recommended low-cost solution that excludes oxygen and halts weevil reproduction without chemical treatment.

*Source: general West African grain legume agronomy literature; cross-referenced from groundnut/millet/sorghum intercropping guides cited elsewhere in this corpus.*
