# Fall Armyworm Management in Maize (Nigeria)

## Identification
Fall armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) larvae cause "window-pane" damage on young maize leaves
and frass (droppings) near the whorl. Early detection in the whorl stage is critical.

## Cultural Control
- Early planting aligned with the start of the rains reduces peak pest pressure.
- Intercropping maize with legumes (e.g. cowpea) can reduce infestation severity.
- Regular field scouting (twice weekly) during the first 6 weeks after emergence.

## Low-cost / Low-input Control
- Hand-removal and crushing of egg masses and young larvae found in the whorl.
- Ash or sand placed in the whorl can physically deter larvae in smallholder plots.
- Neem-based botanical extracts as a lower-cost alternative to synthetic pesticides,
  applied in the late afternoon/evening to reduce UV breakdown.

## Chemical Control (where accessible)
- Use registered insecticides only, following label rates and pre-harvest intervals.
- Rotate active ingredients to slow resistance development.

## When to Seek Extension Support
If infestation exceeds 20% of plants with visible whorl damage at the vegetative stage,
contact a local agricultural extension officer for a field assessment.
