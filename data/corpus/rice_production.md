# Rice Production (Nigeria)

## Lowland vs Upland Rice
Lowland (fadama/swamp) rice is grown in flood-prone or water-retentive areas, while upland rice is rainfed on well-drained soils. Choose fertile land with good water-holding capacity for lowland rice; heavy soils in river valleys are preferred.

## Planting
- **Lowland (transplanted):** transplant 2-3 seedlings per hill at 3-4 cm depth, spaced 20x20 cm (fertile soil/good fertilizer access) up to 30x30 cm (late-maturing varieties, less fertile soil).
- **Lowland (direct seeded/drilled):** sow in rows 20-30 cm apart.
- **Upland:** dibble 5-6 seeds per hole at 20x20 or 30x30 cm spacing, then thin to 3-4 seedlings per stand 2-3 weeks after sowing.
- Plant when rains are firmly established (typically May/June in much of Nigeria); plant early in flood-prone areas to avoid waterlogging at germination.

## Fertilizer
- **Lowland rice:** about 4 bags of NPK 15-15-15/ha applied at sowing/transplanting (basal), plus roughly 2-3 bags of urea/ha as top-dressing at 6-8 weeks after sowing. Some guides recommend splitting the urea top-dressing into two doses (around 3-4 weeks and 6-7 weeks after seeding/transplanting) to reduce nitrogen loss from flooded soils.
- **Upland rice:** similar basal NPK rate (about 4 bags/ha at sowing, fully incorporated into the soil), with urea top-dress at 6-7 weeks.
- Never top-dress while leaves are wet or just before heavy rain — the fertilizer will be lost rather than absorbed.
- A leaf color chart (LCC) can help judge whether additional nitrogen top-dressing is needed.

## Weed and Disease Management
- Weed at 2-3 weeks and again at 5-6 weeks after sowing/transplanting.
- Striga (a parasitic weed) is a problem in some rice fields; manage with Striga-free seed, crop rotation with legumes (soybean, groundnut), intercropping, and timely weeding before the weed produces seed.
- Watch for stem borer "dead-heart" symptoms in early vegetative growth.
- Use resistant/tolerant varieties against rice yellow mottle virus (RYMV) and blast disease where these are problems locally.

## Post-Harvest
- Harvest leaving 15-20 cm of straw to ease hand threshing.
- Sun-dry slowly over 2-3 days to reduce grain breakage during milling; avoid drying directly on bare floors or roadside surfaces, a common source of contamination.
- Store in cool, dry, rodent-proof conditions.

*Sources: IITA "Guide to Rice Production in Northern Nigeria"; Africa Rice Center (WARDA) lowland/upland rice handbooks; NET Training Manual for Rice Production in Nigeria.*
