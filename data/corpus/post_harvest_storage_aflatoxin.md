# Post-Harvest Storage and Aflatoxin Risk

## Why This Matters
Good agronomy can be undone by poor post-harvest handling. Aflatoxin (a toxin produced by Aspergillus fungi) is a serious health and economic risk in maize and groundnut especially, and develops fastest in grain that is poorly dried or stored in damp conditions.

## Drying
- Dry grain on a clean surface (mat, tarpaulin, or concrete floor) — never directly on bare soil or roadside surfaces, which introduces contamination.
- Maize and groundnut should be dried to a safe moisture level (commonly cited around 8-10% for groundnut) before storage; grain that "feels dry" can still be too moist for safe long-term storage.
- Slow, even sun-drying over 2-3 days reduces grain breakage in rice compared to fast drying in one day.

## Storage
- Store in cool, dry, rodent- and insect-proof conditions.
- Hermetic (airtight) storage bags are an effective, widely promoted low-cost technology for protecting grain and legumes from storage pests without chemical fumigants.
- Where fumigation is used (e.g. for rice paddy), it should be done in airtight containers following label instructions, ideally by someone trained in safe pesticide handling.

## Practical Advice for the Assistant to Give
If a farmer describes moldy, discolored, or musty-smelling grain, the safest advice is not to consume or sell it for human or animal feed, since aflatoxin is heat-stable and not destroyed by normal cooking. Recommend testing or discarding visibly affected grain rather than attempting to salvage it.

*Sources: IITA/WARDA rice and groundnut production guides (drying and storage sections).*
